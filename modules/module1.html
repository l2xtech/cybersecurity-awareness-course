Introduction to Information Security

Understanding the fundamentals of protecting information assets

Learning Objectives

What is Information Security?

Information security is the practice of protecting information by mitigating information risks. It includes protecting information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.

In today's digital world, information is one of an organization's most valuable assets. Protecting this information is crucial for maintaining business operations, complying with regulations, and preserving customer trust.

The CIA Triad

The CIA triad is a model designed to guide policies for information security within an organization. It consists of three key components:

Confidentiality

Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.

Integrity

Ensuring information is accurate, complete, and hasn't been improperly modified.

Availability

Ensuring information is accessible when needed by authorized users.

Common Information Security Threats

Malware

Software designed to harm or exploit systems, including viruses, ransomware, and spyware.

Phishing

Deceptive attempts to steal sensitive information by posing as trustworthy entities.

Social Engineering

Manipulating people into divulging confidential information or performing actions.

Weak Passwords

Easy-to-guess passwords that can be exploited through brute force or dictionary attacks.

Your Role in Information Security

Every employee plays a crucial role in maintaining information security. Your daily actions and decisions can either strengthen or weaken the organization's security posture.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Follow security policies and procedures
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication
  • Be vigilant about phishing attempts and suspicious communications
  • Keep software and systems updated
  • Report security incidents promptly
  • Protect sensitive information both online and offline

Connection to ISO 27001

The principles and practices covered in this module align with ISO 27001 requirements, helping our organization maintain compliance while protecting our valuable information assets.
