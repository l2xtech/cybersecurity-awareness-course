What is Information Security?

Information Security (InfoSec) includes practices, policies, and measures to protect information assets from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It focuses on confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

  • Financial records
  • Intellectual property
  • Business strategies
  • Employee information

The CIA Triad

The CIA triad is the foundation of information security:

  • Confidentiality: Authorized access only
  • Integrity: Accuracy and completeness of data
  • Availability: Access when needed

Why Information Security Matters

It's essential to:

  • Protect against threats (cyberattacks, breaches, human error)
  • Ensure business continuity
  • Meet compliance and legal requirements
  • Safeguard organizational reputation
  • Prevent financial losses

Your Role in Information Security

Everyone in the organization has a part to play. You are responsible for:

  • Following security policies
  • Being alert to threats
  • Reporting suspicious activities
  • Protecting credentials and access

One careless action can break even the strongest security system.

Information Security and ISO 27001

ISO 27001 is a global standard for ISMS (Information Security Management System). It covers:

  • Risk assessment and treatment
  • Human resource security
  • Access control
  • Asset management
  • Incident management

Our training aligns with ISO 27001, especially Control 6.3: Awareness.
