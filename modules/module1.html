Module 1: Introduction to Information Security

Learning Objectives

  • Define information security and explain its importance
  • Identify the three main components of the CIA triad
  • Recognize common information security threats
  • Understand your role in maintaining information security
  • Explain how information security relates to ISO 27001

What is Information Security?

Information Security (InfoSec) refers to the practices, policies, and measures designed to protect information assets from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.

Information can include:

  • Customer data
  • Financial records
  • Intellectual property
  • Business strategies
  • Employee information
  • Operational data

The CIA Triad

The foundation of InfoSec is based on:

  • Confidentiality: Only authorized access. (e.g., encryption, access controls)
  • Integrity: Accurate and complete data. (e.g., checksums, version control)
  • Availability: Data/systems are accessible. (e.g., backups, disaster recovery)

Why Information Security Matters

  • Cyberattacks & data breaches
  • Compliance with laws and regulations
  • Business continuity
  • Reputation protection
  • Financial impact

Your Role in InfoSec

You are part of the human firewall.

  • Follow security policies
  • Report suspicious activity
  • Use strong passwords
  • Protect your devices
  • Handle sensitive info responsibly

Information Security and ISO 27001

ISO 27001 provides a framework for implementing and improving information security (ISMS).

  • Risk assessments
  • Security policies and controls
  • Employee awareness (like this training!)
