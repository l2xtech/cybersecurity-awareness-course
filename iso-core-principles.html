Core Principles of ISO 27001

The foundation of ISO 27001 and information security is built on three core principles, often referred to as the "CIA triad": Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. Understanding these principles helps you make better security decisions in your daily work.

Confidentiality

Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.

Daily Examples:

  • Not sharing passwords
  • Locking your screen when away
  • Being careful when discussing sensitive information in public spaces

Integrity

Ensuring information is accurate, complete, and hasn't been improperly modified.

Daily Examples:

  • Verifying data sources before use
  • Following procedures for changing information
  • Reporting any inconsistencies found

Availability

Ensuring information is accessible when needed by authorized users.

Daily Examples:

  • Regularly backing up your work
  • Following recovery procedures
  • Promptly reporting access issues

How These Principles Work Together

These three principles—Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability—form the foundation of information security. Every security measure and practice in our organization is designed to protect one or more of these aspects. When you make decisions about handling information, consider how your actions might impact each of these principles.
