What is ISO 27001?

Simple Explanation

ISO 27001 is an international standard that provides a framework for managing information security. Think of it as a comprehensive guidebook that helps organizations protect their valuable information in a systematic and structured way.

Why It's Important

  • Protects valuable company information from threats
  • Establishes a security-conscious culture throughout the organization
  • Creates consistent processes for handling information
  • Demonstrates commitment to protecting customer and partner data

Benefits for You and the Company

  • A more secure work environment
  • Clear guidance on handling sensitive information
  • Reduced security incidents
  • Increased trust from clients and partners

Core Principles of ISO 27001

Confidentiality

Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.

Daily Examples:
  • Not sharing passwords
  • Locking your screen when away
  • Being careful when discussing sensitive information in public spaces

Integrity

Ensuring information is accurate, complete, and hasn't been improperly modified.

Daily Examples:
  • Verifying data sources before use
  • Following procedures for changing information
  • Reporting any inconsistencies found

Availability

Ensuring information is accessible when needed by authorized users.

Daily Examples:
  • Regularly backing up your work
  • Following recovery procedures
  • Promptly reporting access issues

These three principles—Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (often referred to as the "CIA triad")—form the foundation of information security. Every security measure and practice in our organization is designed to protect one or more of these aspects.

Applying ISO 27001 in Your Daily Work

Practical Tips for Everyone

  • Keep your physical and digital workspace clean and organized
  • Question unusual requests for information
  • Verify people's identity before sharing data
  • Report suspicious behaviors or situations
  • Actively participate in security training

Common Situations and How to Act

When receiving a suspicious email
  • Don't click on suspicious links or attachments
  • Check the sender's email address carefully
  • Report to IT security if you suspect phishing
When working in a public place
  • Use a privacy screen on your laptop
  • Be aware of who might be listening or watching
  • Avoid accessing highly sensitive information
When handling confidential information
  • Follow classification guidelines
  • Store securely when not in use
  • Share only with authorized individuals
When using personal devices for work
  • Follow the company's BYOD policy
  • Keep your device updated and secure
  • Separate work and personal data when possible

Frequently Asked Questions about ISO 27001

What does ISO 27001 mean for me as an employee?

As an employee, ISO 27001 provides guidelines for how to handle information securely in your daily work. It means following established security practices, being vigilant about potential threats, and taking responsibility for protecting company information.

Do I need to know all the technical details of ISO 27001?

No, you don't need to know all the technical details. What's important is understanding the basic principles and how they apply to your specific role and responsibilities. The cybersecurity training modules provide the practical knowledge you need.

How do I know if I'm following ISO 27001 principles correctly?

If you're following the security policies and procedures established by the company, completing required training, and applying the security practices you've learned, you're on the right track. When in doubt, ask your manager or the information security team.

What are my specific responsibilities related to ISO 27001?

Your responsibilities include: following security policies and procedures, completing required security training, protecting information assets you work with, reporting security incidents or concerns, and continuously applying security best practices in your daily work.

How does ISO 27001 relate to other regulations (GDPR, LGPD, etc.)?

ISO 27001 provides a framework for information security that can help organizations comply with various data protection regulations. While ISO 27001 focuses on information security broadly, regulations like GDPR and LGPD focus specifically on personal data protection. Following ISO 27001 practices helps support compliance with these regulations.
